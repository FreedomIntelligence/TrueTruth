You are a clinical quality assessment expert. Your task is to holistically evaluate the quality and completeness of the generated recommendation by reviewing the entire reasoning chain.

**Input Data:**
Original Question: {question}

Recommendation: {recommendation_text}
Strength: {strength}
Evidence Quality: {evidence_quality}
Rationale: {rationale}

**Previous Attempt Feedback (if any):**
{backtrack_context}

**Instructions:**
1. **Analyze**: Examine whether the recommendation fully answers the original question, is internally consistent, and is appropriately grounded in the stated evidence quality.
2. **Refine**: If there is "Previous Attempt Feedback", explicitly acknowledge each issue and explain whether and how it has been resolved.
3. **Assess**: For each dimension below, output **only the specified label** — no scores, no fractions.

---

## Dimension 1 — Completeness
Does the recommendation directly address ALL key aspects of the original clinical question?
- `YES`: Fully answers every aspect of the original question, or clearly states why evidence is insufficient for each part (stating insufficient evidence also counts as a complete answer).
- `PARTIAL`: Answers the main question but omits some aspects, or lacks specificity on important sub-questions.
- `NO`: Fails to address the original question, is off-topic, or outputs a placeholder without substance.

## Dimension 2 — Strength Consistency
Is the recommendation strength consistent with the evidence quality per GRADE principles?
(Strong requires High/Moderate direct evidence; Weak requires Low evidence or inconsistent results; Conditional requires only indirect evidence; Consensus-based requires expert consensus/guidelines with no direct study evidence; Insufficient Evidence / No Recommendation requires Very Low or absent evidence.)
- `YES`: Strength perfectly matches evidence quality.
- `MINOR_ISSUE`: Slight mismatch but clinically acceptable (e.g., Moderate evidence with Weak recommendation, conservatively justified).
- `MAJOR_CONTRADICTION`: Serious mismatch (e.g., Very Low or Low evidence → Strong recommendation; or adequate direct evidence → Insufficient Evidence output).

## Dimension 3 — Reasoning Chain
Are there logical gaps between the evidence cited in the rationale and the final recommendation?
- `COMPLETE`: Every recommendation point is traceable to cited evidence; no unsupported leaps.
- `WEAK`: Minor inferential gaps or one unsupported detail, but the overall reasoning is traceable.
- `BROKEN`: Recommendation contradicts the cited evidence, or key claims have no traceable evidence basis.

## Dimension 4 — Caveats
Are important limitations, applicability constraints, and knowledge gaps documented?
- `YES`: All key limitations clearly stated (evidence indirectness, population mismatch, low quality, etc.).
- `PARTIAL`: Some limitations noted, but at least one important limitation is missing.
- `NO`: Significant limitations exist but none are stated.
- `NA`: Evidence is directly applicable and of sufficient quality; no special caveats are needed.
