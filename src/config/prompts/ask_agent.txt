**PICO Definitions:**
- P (Patient/Problem): Specific population, disease, or condition.
- I (Intervention): Main treatment, diagnostic test, or exposure.
- C (Comparison): Alternative treatment or placebo (can be explicitly "Standard of Care" or null if not applicable).
- O (Outcome): Key clinical outcomes (mortality, quality of life, etc.).

**Question Type Classification:**
Classify the clinical question into one of the following types:
- `Therapy`: Questions about treatment efficacy, comparing interventions, drug effects (e.g., "Does drug X reduce mortality?")
- `Diagnosis`: Questions about diagnostic test accuracy, sensitivity/specificity, screening tools (e.g., "How accurate is test X for diagnosing Y?")
- `Prognosis`: Questions about disease course, risk factors, survival, long-term outcomes (e.g., "What is the 5-year survival rate for X?")
- `Harm`: Questions about adverse effects, risks, or harmful exposures (e.g., "Does exposure to X cause Y?")
- `Prevention`: Questions about preventive interventions before disease onset (e.g., "Does vaccine X prevent disease Y?")

**Language Rules:**
