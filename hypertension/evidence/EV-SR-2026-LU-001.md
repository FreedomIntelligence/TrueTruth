## English Abstract

Longdan Xiegan decoction, a classical formula in traditional Chinese medicine, has been increasingly utilized as an adjunctive therapy for hypertension. This systematic review and meta-analysis aimed to evaluate the efficacy of Longdan Xiegan formula in the adjuvant management of hypertension.

A comprehensive search was conducted in databases including CNKI, Wanfang, VIP, EMBase, Web of Science, Scopus, PubMed, and the Cochrane Library, covering all publications from inception to May 2025. Two reviewers independently carried out study selection, data extraction, and methodological quality evaluation. Eligible for inclusion were randomized controlled trials examining the use of Longdan Xiegan formula in hypertension treatment. Meta-analysis was conducted with RevMan 5.3 software, and evidence quality was assessed according to the Cochrane system.

Nine studies were incorporated, involving 782 participants in total. These participants were randomly assigned to test and control groups. Outcome indicators included effective rate, systolic blood pressure, total cholesterol, triglyceride, heart rate, and traditional Chinese Medicine Symptom Score, etc. Following treatment, the test group showed significantly better indicators than the control group, with all differences being statistically significant (P < .05).

Longdan Xiegan formula demonstrates potential in accelerating symptom alleviation and contributing to the reduction of diverse risk indices in the management of hypertension. Nonetheless, given the scarcity of high quality studies, particularly a dearth of relevant research overseas, subsequent investigations should give precedence to large-sample, double-blind, randomized controlled trials. This will serve to fortify the theoretical framework and yield more compelling evidence for its clinical application.

## 背景 / Background

Hypertension, a chronic, multifactorial medical condition, has emerged as a formidable global health challenge, significantly contributing to the worldwide disease burden and mortality statistics.[1] Characterized by persistently elevated arterial blood pressure, this age-related disorder is not only highly prevalent but also represents a major risk factor for a spectrum of severe cardiovascular and renal complications, including myocardial infarction, stroke, heart failure, and end-stage renal disease.[2] The 21st century has witnessed an alarming escalation in the incidence of hypertension and its associated comorbidities across diverse populations and geographical regions. This trend has highlighted the urgent imperative for enhanced global efforts in the prevention, detection, and management of vascular diseases. In industrialized nations, epidemiological studies have revealed that the cumulative lifetime risk of developing hypertension, defined as a systolic blood pressure (SBP) ≥ 140 mm Hg and/or a diastolic blood pressure ≥ 90 mm Hg, exceeds 90%. The management of hypertension typically necessitates a comprehensive, multifaceted approach. Most patients with hypertension require a combination of 2 or more antihypertensive medications from different pharmacological classes to achieve and maintain blood pressure within the target range. Concurrently, statin therapy is often prescribed to address dyslipidemia, another significant risk factor for cardiovascular disease, thereby reducing the overall cardiovascular risk profile. Despite the availability of a wide range of effective and well-tolerated antihypertensive medications, numerous clinical studies[3] have demonstrated that a substantial proportion of hypertensive patients fail to achieve optimal blood pressure control, and many continue to exhibit elevated levels of associated risk factors, such as hyperglycemia and dyslipidemia. The marked regional variations in the prevalence of hypertension can be attributed to a complex interplay of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors. Among these, dietary factors, including excessive sodium intake and inadequate potassium consumption, play a pivotal role. High sodium diets are associated with increased sodium retention, which in turn leads to volume expansion and elevated blood pressure. Conversely, potassium has been shown to promote sodium excretion and vasodilation, thereby counteracting the hypertensive effects of sodium. Additionally, lifestyle factors such as obesity, alcohol abuse, physical inactivity, and unhealthy dietary patterns, characterized by high intakes of saturated fats, trans fats, and refined sugars, contribute significantly to the development and progression of hypertension.[4,5]

Although hypertension is challenging to cure, appropriate use of antihypertensive medications can significantly reduce its complications.[6,7] Traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) offers unique insights into complex problems that are currently difficult for western medicine to address, and it can sometimes achieve very significant therapeutic effects.[8] Longdan Xiegan decoction is primarily used for treating liver and gallbladder real fire ascending and liver and gallbladder damp-heat descending syndromes. Research has confirmed that it can improve intestinal and liver tissue lesions, blood lipid levels, bile components, and abnormal cholesterol metabolism in cholesterol stone model mice. Its mechanism of action may involve the regulation of NPC1L1 and ABCG5/G8 expression in intestinal and liver tissues.[9,10] A systematic evaluation of its efficacy in hypertensive patients will enhance understanding of its value and provide valuable insights for future adjunctive treatments of hypertension.

## 方法 / Methods

The literature must consist of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) assessing the adjuvant treatment of hypertension patients using Longdan Xiegan decoction, and these studies must be fully documented and published in peer-reviewed journals.

The literature must explicitly describe the diagnostic methods and criteria for hypertension patients in accordance with established guidelines.

The experimental and control groups must exhibit comparable baseline characteristics.

Studies with ambiguous or insufficiently detailed descriptions of the research subject sources.

Studies with incomplete result data, where the reasons for such incompleteness are inadequately explained.

Duplicate publications of the same study across different databases.

Search terms included: “Hypertension,” “Xuan Yun,” “Longdan Xiegan Decoction,” “randomized controlled trial,” “RCT.” Searches were conducted using a combination of subject headings and free text terms. Major domestic and international academic databases such as CNKI, Wanfang, VIP, PubMed, EMBase, Scopus, Cochrane Library, and Web of Science were searched for RCTs on the adjuvant treatment of hypertension patients with Longdan Xiegan decoction. Reference lists of included RCTs were also reviewed. The search timeframe was from the inception of each database to May 2025.

The search strategy suitable for PubMed is as follows:

(“Longdan Xiegan Formula”[Title/Abstract] OR “Longdan Xiegan”[Title/Abstract]).

AND (“Hypertension”[MeSH Terms] OR “hypertension”[Title/Abstract]).

AND (“Controlled Clinical Trials”[MeSH Terms] OR “randomized controlled trial”[Title/Abstract] OR “controlled trial”[Title/Abstract]).

Two researchers independently screened titles and abstracts, excluded duplicates, and excluded studies that did not meet the inclusion criteria. Full texts of potentially eligible studies were retrieved and reviewed. Cross-checking was performed to ensure consistency. Quality assessment was conducted using standardized tools, and any disagreements were resolved by consulting a third researcher. Extracted data included: publication date; inclusion and exclusion criteria; general information about the trial and control groups; interventions; and outcome measures. If included RCTs had incomplete late outcome data, the reason for data loss was identified in the original article.

Two researchers will assess the quality of included RCTs using the “Cochrane Collaboration’s Risk of Bias Assessment Tool.” Specific evaluation items include: random sequence generation, allocation concealment, blinding of participants and personnel, blinding of outcome assessment, completeness of outcome data, selective reporting, and other potential sources of bias.

Meta-analysis was conducted using RevMan 5.3 software. Continuous data were evaluated using mean difference (MD), and categorical data were evaluated using relative risk (RR). Heterogeneity was assessed using the I2 test. For I2 < 25%, indicating no heterogeneity, and a fixed-effect model was applied. For 25% ≤ I2 < 50%, heterogeneity was considered low, still indicating no significant differences, and a fixed-effect model was applied. For 50% ≤ I2 < 75%, heterogeneity was considered moderate, and for I2 ≥ 75%, heterogeneity was considered high, both suggesting significant differences; in these cases, a random-effects model was employed. Sensitivity analyses and subgroup analyses were conducted when feasible to explore potential sources of heterogeneity. Publication bias was assessed using egger test and funnel plots.

## 讨论 / Discussion

A total of 9 RCTs with 782 hypertension patients were included in this meta-analysis, and the results of meta-analysis showed that the improvement of the index levels after treatment in the experimental group were all significantly better than that in the control group. It indicates that the treatment of hypertension patients with Longdan Xiegan formula can promote recovery faster and possesses better overall efficacy. Although the effectiveness based on TCM syndromic scores and those based on blood pressure are not exactly the same, they are essentially the same, which means that Longdan Xiegan formula is perfectly feasible to use it to treat hypertension. And this evaluation is equally inspiring for the future construction of integrated TCM and Western medicine model.

For the other indicators with a middle or high degree of heterogeneity, the source has been known through sensitivity analysis, whereas for the indicator SBP, there is no grouping scheme that fully reveals heterogeneity. However, after analysis, some reasonable guesses that can be given: hypertension inherently behaves differently for different genders and age groups, and Longdan Xiegan formula works differently for different genders and age groups, which may explain the source of heterogeneity.

There is certain limitation in this meta-analysis: The extant RCTs surrounding Longdan Xiegan formula for hypertension are all in the Chinese literature, which is related to the drug’s origins in China, and the fact that the research has emerged in the last few years and is not yet widespread. This means the conclusions of the meta-analysis may be somewhat controversial because of the absence of foreign RCTs, which can be addressed by conducting more clinical studies in the future. In this analysis, we identified indicators with high heterogeneity, which could be well explained by sensitivity analysis. Nevertheless, given the limited number of studies included, conclusions should be drawn with caution.

Longdan Xiegan formula can effectively reduce TC, TG, and blood pressure of patients with real and definite efficacy. The correct application of Longdan Xiegan formula will help to enhance the therapeutic effect and improve the quality of life of hypertensive patients. Longdan Xiegan formula has definite and stable efficacy in the treatment of hypertension. In this formula, Gentiana scabra, with its bitter and cold properties, clears excessive heat in the liver meridian. Bupleurum chinense regulates heat in the Shaoyang Gallbladder meridian. Scutellaria baicalensis and Gardenia jasminoides, both bitter and cold, clear heat and dry dampness. Alisma orientale drains dampness in the kidney meridian. Periploca sepium and Plantago asiatica clear dampness in the small intestine and bladder, promoting diuresis and eliminating heat. These actions help guide damp-heat out through the urinary tract. Angelica sinensis and Rehmannia glutinosa nourish yin, enrich blood, and tonify the liver. Glycyrrhiza uralensis harmonizes all the ingredients. The entire formula works synergistically to clear liver and gallbladder heat, eliminate dampness and heat, and is highly beneficial in the treatment of hypertension.[10]

## 结论 / Conclusion

When treating hypertension, it is important to rule out any identifiable secondary causes of hypertension and implement nonpharmacologic interventions as a first step. These interventions may include exercise, weight loss, and a healthy diet emphasizing a low-sodium diet.[20] In the treatment of hypertension, Western medicine remains more widely utilized. Western medicine offers the advantages of rapid onset and evident efficacy. However, it also carries risks such as potential side effects and drug dependence.[21] From the perspective of traditional Chinese medicine, Longdan Xiegan decoction exerts effects of clearing liver fire and eliminating damp-heat, thereby calming the hyperactive liver yang that disturbs the upper body and improving blood pressure-related symptoms. From the perspective of modern pharmacological research, gentian, S baicalensis and other components in its formula can assist in lowering blood pressure by regulating vasomotor function, improving lipid metabolism, inhibiting inflammatory responses and other pathways, and it has definite curative effects.[22,23] Currently, the number of relevant studies is relatively small, all of which are small-sample, single-center trials. In the future, more large-sample, multicenter trials should be conducted to better evaluate its therapeutic efficacy.
