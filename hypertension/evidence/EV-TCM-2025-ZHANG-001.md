## English Abstract

Hypertension is a major risk factor for cardiovascular disease, kidney disease, and stroke. Xuefu Zhuyu decoction (Xuefu Zhuyu prescription [XZP]) is a classic Chinese medicine prescription, which has been widely used in headache, vertigo, hypertension, and so on. This meta-analysis aimed at evaluating the efficacy and safety of XZP for hypertension and related risk diseases of hypertension, especially cardiovascular disease, with sufficient evidence.

A comprehensive literature search was performed in 7 electronic databases from their establishment up to April 7, 2022, to examine the therapeutic effect and safety of XZP for hypertension. The primary outcome included systolic blood pressure (BP), diastolic BP, categorical BP, traditional Chinese medicine syndrome integral, and traditional Chinese medicine syndrome effect. The secondary outcomes were defined as vascular endothelial function, ventricular remodeling, and blood lipids. Poor prognosis included stroke, heart failure, and so on. Continuous variables were analyzed using mean difference and 95% confidence intervals. Relative risk and 95% confidence interval were used for dichotomous data. The data were pooled and analyzed by RevMan5.4.

A total of 37 studies with 3391 hypertensive patients were included for meta-analysis. All studies compared XZP and antihypertensive or lipid-lowering drugs with conventional treatment used alone. Overall, 25 studies demonstrated that XZP substantially reduced BP compared with the control group (P < .00001). A total of 11 studies showed that XZP significantly improved symptoms in patients with hypertension (P < .00001). Meanwhile, this result indicated that XZP has good effects on lowering blood lipids, improving vascular endothelial function, ventricular remodeling, and carotid intima-media thickness, so it can reverse the damage of hypertensive target organs and reduce the incidence of related risk diseases (P < .05). Encouragingly, the incidences of adverse reactions and adverse outcomes were also lower than with conventional treatment. The Z curves for the key outcome indicators cross the traditional thresholds and the trial sequential analysis boundaries, indicating that the conclusions are reliable.

XZP shows good clinical efficacy in simultaneously reducing BP, protecting the cardiovascular system, and preventing related risk diseases of hypertension. XZP may provide a multiefficacy therapeutic method in the prevention and treatment of hypertension and its associated risk factors.

## 背景 / Background

Hypertension is an increasingly distinguished global public health problem, which is the leading cause of cardiovascular mortality and morbidity.[1] In 2019, the global age-standardized prevalence of hypertension in adults aged 30 to 79 years was 34% in men and 32% in women. At the same time, due to population growth and aging, the absolute number of people with hypertension doubled from 648 million people in 1990 to 1278 million people in 2019.[2] Meanwhile, the association between elevated blood pressure (BP) and cardiovascular disease (CVD), chronic kidney disease, and all-cause mortality is powerful, independent, and straight. Hypertension is one of the most preventable and modifiable risk factors that can be restrained through medication and a healthy lifestyle.[3] Therefore, antihypertensive therapy is highly valued in preventing hypertension-related risks, especially cardiovascular risks.[4,5] Over the past 40 years, the estimated mean BP has remained constant or decreased slightly worldwide, which is thanks to the use of antihypertensive medications. However, the treatment and safety of today’s treatment are not ideal. Hence, extra therapeutic approaches with multiple benefits and high safety gained increasing popularity worldwide.

Traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) is a theoretical system with 1000 years of clinical experience. Through the rational compatibility of medicine under the guidance of TCM theory, Chinese medicinal formulae have the characteristics of individual adjustment of multiple components and targets with minor side effects. Xuefu Zhuyu prescription (XZP) is a classic Chinese medicine prescription, which was recorded in the classic medical book Yi Lin Gai Cuo by Wang Qingren in the Qing Dynasty. This prescription is mainly composed of drugs promoting blood circulation and removing blood stasis, including safflower flower (Flos Carthami Tinctorii, Honghua), peach kernel (Taoren, Persicae Semen), Chinese angelica root (Danggui, Radix Angelicae Sinensis), Rehmannia (Di Huang, Radix Rehmanniae Glutinosae), red peony root (Chi Shao, Radix Rubrus Paeoniae Lactiflorae), Szechuan lovage root (Chuanxiong, Rhizoma Ligustici Chuanxiong), Achyranthes root (Niu Xi, Achyranthis Bidentatae Radix), root of the balloon flower (Jiegeng, Platycodi Radix), orange fruit (Zhike, Fructus Aurantii), Thorowax root (Chaihu, Radix Bupleuri).

The mechanism of XZP in the treatment of hypertension lies in reducing blood viscosity, protecting vascular endothelial cells,[6] regulating endothelial cell function, inhibiting vascular remodeling,[7] blocking myocardial fibrosis,[8] inhibiting the renin–angiotensin–aldosterone system,[9] improving microcirculation, and dilating blood vessels.[10] Moreover, XZP can also regulate lipid metabolism and is not easy to cause bleeding.[11] At the same time, it also has the property of multitarget, which can produce a series of corresponding biological effects.[12,13] This is consistent with the characteristics of frequent pathogenesis and multisystem damage of hypertension and provides more multifaceted treatment methods for hypertension.

Although XZP has the advantages mentioned in the above theoretical research, we still need to conduct a system assessment and meta-analysis of clinical research. A previous meta-analysis of XZP provided a preliminary assessment of XZP. However, due to the limited number of trials included, the small range of evaluation, and the limited methodological quality, some conclusions cannot be confirmed as valid. In recent years, the randomized controlled trials (RCTs) of XZP for hypertension have increased, and the evaluation of hypertension is becoming more and more comprehensive. Thus, a reassessment that is more comprehensive and sufficient evidence was timely and necessary. To assess the effectiveness and safety of XZP on hypertension, this study not only evaluated the BP and symptoms of XZP for hypertension patients but also reviewed the time of reduction of pressure, the change of the related factors of hypertension, the incidence of adverse reactions and the occurrence of the terminal events.

## 方法 / Methods

This systematic review is conducted following the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses: The PRISMA Statement. This study has been registered, and the PROSPERO number is CRD42022338579.

Only RCTs were included in this systematic review, regardless of blinding, publication status, or language. Animal studies were not considered.

Only hypertensive patients were included. No restriction on age, sex, or ethnicity was predefined. Hypertension should be diagnosed clinically according to the criteria documented in the 7th report of the Joint National Committee or other guidelines and definitions. Studies would be excluded if hypertensive patients also had severe systemic problems, such as liver and kidney failure, and heart failure.

RCTs that evaluated the effect of XZP or XZP combined with antihypertensive or lipid-lowering drugs (ALD) with ALD for hypertension. Patients in the control group were treated with conventional Western medicine, and patients in the treatment group were treated with XZP or XZP combined with conventional Western medicine, and the types and delivery methods of drugs used were the same as those in the control group. Studies excluded other complementary and alternative medicine, and therapies beyond XZP, including Taijiquan, acupuncture, moxibustion, massage, yoga, and other TCM. In addition, studies with obvious errors and duplicate publications reporting the same conclusions were excluded.

Lowering BP is an important cornerstone of hypertension treatment, so we selected systolic blood pressure (SBP), diastolic blood pressure (DBP), and categorical BP as the main outcomes. In addition, the symptoms of hypertension greatly troubled many hypertensive patients, and sometimes did not improve with the decrease in BP. Consequently, TCM syndrome integral (TCMSI), and TCM syndrome effect (TCMSE) were also used as the main outcomes. The standard of categorical BP and the standard of TCMSI are mentioned in the guiding principles for clinical research of new chinese medicine (GPRC). Categorical BP was divided into 3 categories: all clinical symptoms disappeared, DBP decreased > 1.33 kPa, and SDP decreased > 2.67 kPa was significant. The clinical symptoms were effectively alleviated and DBP level decreased < 1.33 kPa, and SBP level decreased < 2.67 kPa, which was called effective. Clinical symptoms have not been improved, and DBP level and SBP levels have not been reduced or even increased, it is invalid. TCM syndromes are divided into primary symptoms (dizziness and headache) and secondary symptoms (chest distress, heart palpitations, and shortness of breath). The 4-grade scoring method is used to carry out quantitative integration. The primary symptom grade of 0 is 0 points, without symptoms. Grade I is 2, with occasional seizures and occasional discomfort. Grade II was 4, with frequent attacks. The score of grade III was 6, and the score of grade 0 was 0, without symptoms. Grade I is 1, with occasional seizures and occasional discomfort. Grade II was 2, with frequent attacks; level III is 3 points. Each TCMSI is added to calculate the total score. The higher the score is, the more serious the symptom is. The secondary outcomes were defined as vascular endothelial function (VEF), ventricular remodeling (VR), blood lipids, carotid intima-media thickness (IMT), the incidence of adverse events, and the incidence of poor prognosis.

Relevant publications evaluating the effect of XZP on hypertension were searched in PubMed, EMBASE, Web of Science, the Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials, the Chinese National Knowledge Infrastructure, the Chinese Biomedical Literature Database, and the Wanfang Database from their inception up to April 7, 2022. The following grouped terms were used as a search strategy and modified to suit each database:(“hypertension” OR “hypertension” OR “blood pressure, high” OR “blood pressure, high” OR “blood pressures, high” OR “high blood pressure” OR “high blood pressure” OR “high blood pressure”) AND (“randomized controlled trial” OR “randomized controlled trial” OR “randomized controlled trials as topic” OR “randomized controlled trials as topic” OR “clinical trials, randomized” OR “trials, randomized clinical” OR “controlled clinical trials, randomized”) AND (“Xue-fu-zhu-yu decoction” OR “xfzy decoction” OR “xuefu zhuyu” OR “xuefu zhuyu”). We also searched the ongoing experiments through the international clinical trial registration website hosted by the National Institutes of Health (http://www.clinicaltrials.gov) and the China clinical trial registry (http://www.chictr.org.cn) to retrieve any unpublished documents. The references of identified studies and previous meta-analysis articles were also searched. No restriction on the publication language and status in our literature search.

The titles and abstracts of all the selected articles were independently screened by 2 reviewers according to the eligibility criteria listed above. Duplicate publications were removed accordingly. Afterward, full texts of potentially relevant articles were retrieved for further assessment. Disagreements were resolved by consultation with a 3rd reviewer.

Basic information of the eligible studies was extracted by 2 reviewers independently using a standardized data extraction form. The extracted points included the following: basic information of the studies – title, authors’ name, publication time, country of publication, and publication type; basic characteristics of the enrolled patients – age, sexuality, course of the disease, sample size, study location, diagnosis criteria of hypertension and TCM syndrome, baseline difference, and BP before the treatment; basic characteristics of the studies – study design, interventions in the treatment and control groups, specific recipe composition, dosage and administration methods of XZP-based formula, and duration of therapy; primary and secondary outcome measures – SBP, DBP, categorical BP, TCMSI, TCMSE, VEF, ventricular reconstruction, blood lipids, IMT, the incidence of adverse events, and incidence of poor prognosis. The correspondence authors of the included studies were contacted by email, fax, and telephone number to obtain the missing data.

The risk of bias in each study was independently evaluated by 2 reviewers according to the Cochrane collaboration tool.[14] It involved the following 7 domains: adequate sequence generation; allocation concealment; blinding of participants and personnel; blinding of outcome assessment; incomplete outcome data; selective reporting; and other bias. Each domain was ranked as “yes” (low risk of bias), “no” (high risk of bias), or “unclear” (unclear risk of bias).

Data analysis was performed using the Review Manager software (Review Manager [RevMan] [Computer program], Version 5.4; The Cochrane Collaboration, 2020, Cochrane International Network, England). Outcome measures after treatment were presented as weighted mean difference (WMD) with 95% confidence interval (CI) for continuous outcomes, and risk ratio (RR) with 95% CI for dichotomous outcomes. Heterogeneity of effect sizes was tested using the I2 statistics. A random-effects model was adopted to assess the effects of the XZP-based formula for hypertension across trials if substantial heterogeneity was observed (I2 > 50% or P < .1); otherwise, a fixed-effects model was used. A funnel plot was used to examine the publication bias. P < .05 was considered significant. TSA 0.9.5.10 beta software (available at http://www.ctu.dk; Copenhagen Trial Unit, Copenhagen, Denmark) was used to analyze the trial series of outcome indicators, calculate and produce Z score plots to reduce the incidence of random errors, judge the reliability of the conclusions, and estimate the sample size required for the meta-analysis.

## 结果 / Results

According to the retrieval method mentioned above, 47 potential related articles were found, and 198 duplicate publications were excluded. A total of 188 articles were excluded by reading titles and abstracts because they were meta-analyses, summaries of clinical experience, questionnaires, meetings, letters, cohort studies, animal studies, and RCT studies inconsistent with the research topic. The remaining 47 studies were then read in full for qualification assessment. A total of 10 trials were excluded for the following reasons: 3 did not meet the inclusion criteria; 3 were published repeatedly; 4 were academic papers. ## 讨论 / Discussion

In adults with hypertension, the use of antihypertensive medications could control BP, which reduces the risk of CVD and all-cause mortality to some extent. Nevertheless, using ALD alone sometimes cannot improve the uncomfortable symptoms of patients with hypertension, and the adverse reactions and side effects of drugs are inevitable.[62] For this reason, it is still a major task for hypertension treatment to improve treatment methods and explore more safe and effective treatment methods that can prevent CVD, chronic kidney disease, encephalopathy, and other related diseases. XZP is composed of 11 TCMs such as peach kernel and safflower, which has unique advantages in the treatment of hypertension due to their multitarget comprehensive action. Peach kernel and Angelica have anticoagulant and antithrombotic effects, which can improve myocardial ischemia injury and cerebral vascular activity.[63,64] Safflower has the effect of dilating coronary arteries, which can improve arterial blood flow, reduce vascular resistance, and prevent platelet aggregation and thrombosis, such therapeutic effects can influence the expression of a variety of cytokines, so it can play a role in multiple systems, organs, and diseases.[4,42] Wang et al[65] examined the effects of XZP on factors such as BP, symptoms, and lipids in a meta-analysis. Our updated meta-analysis offers a reliable assessment of XZP for hypertension and associated risks. Compared with prior studies, ours was superior in terms of strict criteria, rigorous screening, multiple included trials, and comprehensive outcomes, with more reliable results. We incorporated a larger number of patients and randomized trials, extended the follow-up time, and included earlier trials. To comprehensively evaluate XZP for hypertension and related risks, especially cardiovascular risks, we considered not only BP, symptoms, antihypertensive time, and adverse reactions but also target organ injury and poor prognosis events. Through subgroup analysis, we explored the effects of treatment duration and dosage forms on BP, as well as the link between efficacy and patient age. Overall, our results are more robust and evidence-based than previous meta-analyses. They can help determine XZP’s efficacy in hypertension and related factors and inspire new hypotheses for future randomized trials.

A total of 37 studies involving 3391 patients met the inclusion criteria for this study. In brief, XZP has superior and diversified therapeutic effects in the adjuvant treatment of hypertension, and is more effective than the use of ALD alone in reducing BP, improving symptoms, improving blood lipids, protecting VEF, preventing VR, reducing IMT level, reducing adverse reactions, and preventing related diseases.

Hypertension is an important, preventable, and remediable risk factor of globalization. The goal of hypertension treatment is to achieve ideal cardiovascular health.[62] Therefore, hypertension treatment should be used to prevent the occurrence of cardiovascular and other hypertension-related diseases based on BP control. In our research, we have the following advantages. First, XZP has obvious advantages in BP control, which lays a solid foundation for the comprehensive prevention and treatment of hypertension with XZP. SBP and DBP were reported in 25 studies (25/37, 68%). XZP reduced SBP by 11.48 mm Hg and DBP by 5.84 mm Hg, compared with antihypertensive or lipid-lowering agents alone. In addition, 20 trials (20/37, 54%) reported categorical BP, which demonstrated that XZP had significantly better antihypertensive efficacy than conventional treatment. This is consistent with the results of previous meta-analyses. Compared with previous studies, more RCTS and cases were included, and more antihypertensive results were obtained, which further confirms the efficacy of XZP and reflects that the antihypertensive ability of XZP is still worthy of further exploration. The above results were statistically significant. TSA further affirmed the efficacy of XZP.

We performed subgroup analyses for different treatment durations, dosage forms, and patient ages of XZP in the process of integrating analysis. In the subgroup analysis of treatment time, the number of studies in both groups was more than 10. Studies have shown that both long-term and short-term use of XZP are effective in lowering BP, but the antihypertensive effects of more than 3 months of use were not as good as short-term use. It was suggested that the antihypertensive efficacy of XZP did not increase with the extension of time of use, which may be caused by the complex pathogenesis of hypertension or research bias. The relationship between the duration of XZP use and antihypertensive efficacy and its causes still needs to be further studied. In addition, our subgroup analysis of dosage form showed that Chinese medicine decoction of XZP reduced SBP by 12.08 mm Hg more than conventional drug treatment, while the antihypertensive effect of Chinese patent medicine XZP showed no statistically significant difference with the control group, and there was no significant difference in DBP reduction between the 2 groups. In the age subgroup analysis, it was interesting to find that the antihypertensive effect of XZP in older adults was superior to that in hypertensive patients under 60 years of age, which rarely occurs with other antihypertensive medications. Superior age is an independent risk factor for hypertension and related risk diseases, and the treatment of hypertension in the elderly is a great challenge. Our results prove that XZP is a potential preponderant drug for the treatment of hypertension in the elderly.

It is worth noting that headache, dizziness, nausea, and other uncomfortable symptoms in some cases seriously troubled many patients with hypertension, greatly affecting the quality of life of patients.[66] Even if taking ALD, many patients with hypertension still cannot get rid of the uncomfortable symptoms caused by hypertension. In our study, the uncomfortable symptoms of patients with hypertension were scored in a standard way, and the symptoms of patients were quantified according to the degree and severity of the association between symptoms and hypertension by TCMSI and TCMSE. TCMSI was reported in 11 trials, and comprehensive analysis showed that XZP significantly improved hypertension symptoms.

The coexisting risk factors of hypertension should also be paid attention to in the treatment of hypertension. In recent years, some clinical studies have been carried out on the effects of Xuefu Zhuyu decoction on hemodynamics and blood lipid, and relatively positive conclusions have been drawn. Xuefu Zhuyu decoction also has the pharmacological effects of regulating blood lipid and antilipid oxidation.[67,68] Lipid control can not only prevent atherosclerosis but also have a positive effect on reducing the incidence of hypertension.[63,64] Meanwhile, blood lipid is also an important factor affecting the occurrence and development of cardiovascular diseases, so reducing blood lipid has been paid more and more attention to the prevention and treatment of hypertension and related diseases. Our study showed that XZP significantly increased TC, TG, LDL, and OX-LDL levels in hypertensive patients, and decreased by 1.98, 1.46, 1.11, and 9.08 mmol/L, respectively. At the same time, HDL-C increased by 0.15 mmol/L but was not statistically significant. In addition, the samples of the blood of the XZP group also improved greatly.

Furthermore, we evaluated XZP’s treatment of hypertension and its associated risks, especially cardiovascular risk prevention, and the overall results were significantly advantageous. Prevention and reversal of target organ injury is an important part of hypertension management and has a significant impact on the prevention of overall risk. Sustained hypertension can cause damage to the heart, vascular endothelium, carotid artery, kidney, brain, and other target organs, manifested as left ventricular hypertrophy, increased urinary albumin, increased carotid intima, and middle thickness, pulse wave conduction velocity, cerebrovascular lesions, and so on. These injuries are considered strong predictors of cardiovascular and other diseases.[4] Therefore, the evaluation of hypertension also includes changes in the heart, vascular endothelium, carotid artery, pulse wave conduction velocity, and so on. The results showed that XZP had a significant improvement effect on heart rate, LVEF, LVESD, LVEDD, VTId, VTIs, IMT, FMD, ET-1, VOI, BaPWV, hS-CRP, soluble vascular cell adhesion molecule-1, ET-1, and serum endogenous secretion of RAGE in hypertensive patients, and all had statistical significance. In addition, NMD, TXB2, 6-keto-PGF1α, IVST, LVMI, and other indicators were also improved in the XZP group, but there was no statistical difference. What is noteworthy is that the incidence of hypertension-related risk diseases has also been reported. The results showed that the incidence of stroke, heart failure, hospitalization, gastrointestinal reactions, and gastrointestinal bleeding was lower in the XZP group than in the conventional drug group. However, due to the limited observation time, the incidence of hypertension-risk diseases may be different from the actual situation.

A previous meta-analysis of adverse reactions to XZP for hypertension reported 3 patients with nausea and 2 patients with dry cough, with no serious adverse reactions reported. In this study, adverse effects of XZP were observed in 10 studies, of which only 5 studies reported adverse effects during the use of XZP. The adverse reactions of XZP were significantly less than those of conventional drug therapy alone. However, the meta-analysis showed no statistical difference, indicating that XZP did not increase the incidence of adverse reactions. Moreover, research findings have indicated that the application of XZP in treatment is linked to a reduced incidence of adverse prognostic events. This serves as additional evidence for its efficacy in both the prevention and management of hypertension and its associated risk factors. The overall results indicate that XZP is a potentially effective, safe, and widely used hypertension treatment agent.

The following limitations should be considered before accepting the conclusions. First, due to language limitations, we searched 7 databases in both Chinese and English, but no databases in other languages. Second, the trials included in this study were of limited quality. We evaluated the methodologic quality of the included trials based on the Cochrane collaboration tool. However, all RCTs were conducted in a center in China, most of the studies did not report the blinding method and allocation hiding, and a few of the studies had data missing, which posed challenges to our research results. Third, this study can only evaluate XZP combined with ALD or lipid-lowering drugs in the treatment of hypertension, and cannot prove the efficacy of XZP alone in the treatment of hypertension. Therefore, we can only verify the role of XZP as an adjunctive treatment of hypertension. Fourth, there is a certain publication bias in the XZP treatment of hypertension, as many negative results are not likely to be published. Finally, the potential limitations of this study are not completely consistent with the study design, study type, and sample size in the included trials and slight differences in measurement methods used in different trials may increase other biases in the study.

## 结论 / Conclusion

In conclusion, XZP can not only improve BP and clinical symptoms of patients with hypertension but also improve blood lipid, VEF, VR status, IMT, heart rate, hypersensitive C-reactive protein, and Ang II. Although there may be methodological flaws and bias risks in our study, it may undermine the positive results of XZP in the treatment of hypertension. However, XZP can indeed treat hypertension and improve the prognosis of patients with hypertension. In addition, the relationship between different dosage forms and treatment duration of XZP and the efficacy of hypertension still needs to be further studied. Our study found that XZP has extensive and good therapeutic effects on target organ damage of hypertension, especially for cardiovascular-related damage, which is consistent with the advanced concept of hypertension treatment and management. The use of only 1 drug can have a comprehensive positive impact on hypertension and its related risk factors, especially the indicators of CVD, which is of great significance for the overall management of hypertension, patient compliance improvement, and improving the short-term and long-term efficacy of hypertension. In conclusion, XZP provides a good way for stereoscopic effective treatment and control of hypertension and also leads us to pay more attention to the comprehensive regulation of Chinese herbal medicine for complex diseases.
