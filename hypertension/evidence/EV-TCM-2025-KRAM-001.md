## English Abstract

Serotonin (5-hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT) causes a sustained reduction in mean arterial pressure (MAP) during chronic (one week) infusion in both normo- and hypertensive rats. We hypothesized that the hypotensive effect of 5-HT will remain intact in male Dahl-SS rats that are unresponsive to a diuretic. Male Dahl-SS rats on a normal or 4% NaCl diet were instrumented with radiotelemeters for blood pressure measurement. Following the collection of baseline MAP, Alzet mini-pumps containing 5-HT (25 ug/kg/min) were implanted. On the same day as pump implantation, rats were either: 1) switched to drinking water containing the diuretic trichlormethiazide (TCM- 250 mg/L) or 2) maintained on regular drinking water. Following a week of infusion, blood samples were collected, and standard blood chemistry panels were run to investigate any potential effects on liver, kidney, or cardiometabolic parameters as well as electrolyte balance. On normal salt after 1 week of infusion, rats receiving both 5-HT and TCM had a significantly lower MAP (122.0 ±3.3 mmHg; n=5) than rats receiving 5-HT alone (p=0.0030, 131±2.7 mmHg; n=5). After 1 week of infusion, high salt diet rats receiving both 5-HT and TCM had the greatest reduction in MAP (−20.7±1.7 mmHg; n=4). 5-HT administration did not modify kidney or liver function as assessed by measurements of plasma in rats on normal salt diet. Collectively, these data suggest 5-HT may be an effective additive to current front-line anti-hypertensive medications. Furthermore, 5-HT remains effective even when drug resistance develops to at least one of those standard monotherapies (thiazide diuretics).

5-HT is an effective anti-hypertensive agent in Dahl-SS rats that are non-responsive to a diuretic when placed on a high salt (4%) diet.5-HT may be a viable treatment option for hypertension that is resistant to currently approved therapeutics.

## 背景 / Background

Control of blood pressure presents a global public health challenge. Hypertension is a leading risk factor for mortality and cardiovascular disease. Despite global efforts to manage hypertension, controlling blood pressure has proven difficult, with only 21% of diagnosed adults having their hypertension controlled1–2. In addition to this, there are refractory and resistant forms of hypertension. Resistant hypertension (RH) is defined as the failure to meet a BP goal with adherence to 3 antihypertensive agents3. Current medications include diuretics, angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors or angiotensin receptor blockers (ARBs), beta-blockers, and calcium channel blockers (CCBs)3. With the prevalence of hypertension and the burden it poses to human health, it is clear that our understanding of hypertension and mechanisms by which to control it need to expand.

Serotonin (5-hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT) is a vasoactive amine that has biological functions in many of the tissues and organs that contribute to the regulation of blood pressure. It was originally discovered in the serum and identified as being able to alter smooth muscle tone and is perhaps best known as a vasoconstrictor6. Even so, the effects of 5-HT in the cardiovascular system have yet to be deeply studied, in contrast to the well-studied functions in the central nervous system. The effects of 5-HT in the vasculature are complicated by its ability to activate multiple receptors in multiple tissue types. Despite its being known as a vasoconstrictor, 5-HT can indeed reduce blood pressure7–15. Exogenously infused 5-HT caused a sustained reduction in blood pressure through the reduction of total peripheral resistance (TPR)16. 5-HT reduced TPR through vasodilation as mediated by direct activation of the 5-HT7 receptor16. The vasodilator properties of 5-HT make it a potential candidate for the treatment of hypertension.

We sought to understand the ability of 5-HT to induce hypotension in the presence of a current frontline anti-hypertensive medication. These are medications to which patients can be resistant or refractory. We hypothesized that the hypotensive effect of 5-HT would remain intact in male Dahl SS rats that are unresponsive to a diuretic. Specifically, thiazide diuretics are one such front line medication and among the most commonly prescribed to those with hypertension18. They function in the distal tubule and induce diuresis through inhibition of the Na+-Cl− symporter19–20. It is unclear whether 5-HT-induced hypotension would still occur in a drug-resistant form of hypertension. There are many rodent models of hypertension, however none of them are truly a drug-resistant model21. We chose to study a model that approaches a resistant hypertension, the Dahl SS rat on a high salt diet22. We expected 5-HT mediated vasodilation coupled with a reduction in blood volume by a thiazide diuretic to result in a reduction in blood pressure23.

The present study supports that the hypotensive effect of 5-HT remains intact in male Dahl SS rats that are unresponsive to a diuretic.

## 方法 / Methods

Procedures using animals were in compliance with the institutional guidelines on animal use outlined by Michigan State University (MSU) and the NIH Guidelines on Use of Laboratory Animals. Procedures used in this study were approved by the MSU Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (PROTO202400316). Male Dahl SS rats were either purchased and used directly from Charles River Laboratory, Inc., (Kingston, NY, USA, RRID: RGD 2308886) or were bred from purchased Charles River Dahl SS M and F rats at MSU. Dahl SS rats (~400g at telemeter implantation) were used for telemetry studies. Animals were housed singly on a 12-hour light/dark cycle at 22–25°C and given ad libitum access to either a standard chow diet (Envigo Teklad Diet 2918, 0.2% NaCl, 0.6% K+, irradiated 6.2% fat, 18.6% protein) or a 4% NaCl diet (Research Diets, DI7013). The 4% diet was administered following telemeter implantation and given for three weeks before treatment intervention to allow for the development of hypertension.

Radiotelemeters (model S10; Data Sciences International, MN USA) were implanted via the femoral artery with the catheter advanced into the abdominal aorta. Following a one-week recovery period, the radiotelemeters recorded cardiovascular and physiological measurements every 10 minutes for 10 seconds for the duration of the study and are presented as a 24-hour average.

After one-week of baseline blood pressure measurement, animals were started on a diuretic. Animals were given ad-libitum access to 1) regular drinking water or 2) water containing trichlormethiazide (VWR 76789–360) (250 mg/L). Bottles were weighed daily during administration (figure S4, S5).

After one-week of baseline blood pressure measurement animals were implanted with Alzet 2ML1 mini-pumps containing 5-HT at a dose of 25 ug/kg/min, the maximal effective dose of 5-HT that causes a hypotension. Pumps were placed subcutaneously and removed after one-week of infusion.

After one-week of treatment animals were euthanized in compliance with the institutional guidelines on animal use outlined by Michigan State University (MSU). Animals were anesthetized using 5% isoflurane/O2 and a thoracotomy was performed. At the time of euthanasia body weights were measured, whole blood was collected and heart, liver, and tissue weights measured.

Whole blood was collected at euthanasia; Blood was centrifuged at 13,000 rpm for 10 minutes and plasma collected. Plasma was analyzed using IDEXX blood chemistry CLIPs for basic clinical panels.

Numerical data were graphed using GraphPad Prism (v 10.4.2; GraphPad Software San Diego, CA, USA, RRID: SCR_002798). Values are reported as means ± SEM for the number of animals indicated (n). Blood pressure data are reported as a 24-hour average of readings taken for 10 seconds every 10 minutes throughout the duration of the study.

All values are reported as means ± SEM for the number of animals indicated (n). Graph Pad Prism (v 10.4.2) was used to conduct statistical tests. A two-way ANOVA with a Tukey correction was used to compare the multiple treatment groups. p<0.05 was considered statistically significant. Negative area under the curve (AUC) analysis of the change in blood pressure from baseline was performed for each subject individually (figure S1). These values were then grouped by treatment and analyzed using a one-way ANOVA. A Tukey correction was used to compare the multiple treatment groups a p<0.05 was considered statistically significant. Simple linear regressions were performed to analyze the rate of fall in blood pressure in each treatment group. A one sample t and Wilcoxon test was performed to analyze change in blood pressure (mmHg x day) from baseline. A p<0.05 was considered statistically significant.

## 结果 / Results

In male Dahl SS rats on a standard normal salt diet, subcutaneous infusion of 5-HT (25 ug/kg/min) either alone or co-administered with trichlormethiazide (TCM, 250 mg/L) reduced peak mean arterial pressure (MAP) to a greater degree initially than administration of TCM alone. The 2-day fall in MAP from baseline was −27.4 ± 2.5 mmHg in rats receiving 5-HT and TCM, −26.9 ±1.8 mmHg in rats receiving 5-HT alone, and −14.3 ± 1.0 mmHg in rats receiving TCM alone respectively (figure 1A,1B). The rate at which blood pressure fell during protocol days 7–9 in rats treated with 5-HT and TCM (slope= −3.31 mmHg x day) was greater than those treated with TCM alone (−1.70 mmHg x day) and 5-HT alone (−1.60 mmHg x day). Rats treated with 5-HT reached a maximal hypotensive effect after 2 days of treatment (protocol days 7–9, figure S1) whereas rats treated with TCM alone did not reach a maximal blood pressure nadir until after 4 days of treatment (figure 1B).

All rats treated with 5-HT exhibited an initial tachycardia that waned within two days (figure S2, S3). After one-week of treatment, infusion of 5-HT alone reduced MAP to a similar degree as administration of TCM alone with reductions of −11.2 ± 3.5 mmHg and −12.6 ± 1.3 mmHg at the end of treatment respectively (figure 2A). Co-administration of 5-HT and TCM resulted in a greater magnitude of fall in MAP of −16.1 ± 4.9 mmHg after a week of infusion than either treatment alone, though not statistically significant. The MAP of rats treated with 5-HT and TCM was significantly lower on the final day of treatment when compared to the MAP of rats treated with 5-HT alone. (figure 1A, 2A, * p=0.01).

Rats treated with 5-HT and TCM (145 ±12 mmHg x day) had a significantly greater negative area under the curve by one way-ANOVA compared to rats treated with TCM alone (74.4± 3.1 mmHg x day) but did not differ significantly from rats treated with 5-HT alone (111±12 mmHg x day), (figure 2B, ** p=0.0025). These data indicate that administration of a diuretic with 5-HT enhances 5-HT’s hypotensive effects.

In Dahl SS rats, administration of a 4% NaCl diet for 3 weeks resulted in an average increase in MAP of 13.1±1.5 mmHg. Subcutaneous 7-day infusion of 5-HT alone (25 ug/kg/min) resulted in a greater initial fall in MAP (−36.5 ± 3.0 mmHg) than administration of TCM (−7.6 ± 2.2 mmHg) alone, while co-administration resulted in the largest initial fall in MAP (−46.7 ± 4.3 mmHg) (figure 3A, B).

One week of treatment with TCM alone resulted in a reduction in MAP of −5.8 ± 3.3 mmHg that was not significantly different from baseline (p=0.394), whereas a week treatment with 5-HT alone resulted in a sustained reduction of MAP of −9.1 ± 4.6 mmHg (figure 3A, * p=0.034). A week of co-administration of 5-HT and TCM resulted in a significantly greater reduction in MAP of −20.7 ± 1.7 mmHg (** p=0.008) when compared to treatment with TCM alone (figure 3B, 4A).

Rats treated with 5-HT and TCM had a significantly greater negative area under the curve (237± 17 mmHg x day) by one way-ANOVA compared to rats treated with TCM alone (61.4±13.9 mmHg x day), or 5-HT alone (151±12 mmHg x day) (figure 4B, **** p=<0.0001, ** p=0.0057). Additionally, rats treated with 5-HT alone had a significantly greater negative area under the curve by one way-ANOVA compared to rats treated with TCM alone (figure 4B, ** p=0.0048).

Clinical parameters were measured in plasma from Dahl SS rats on normal diet (normal salt) after a one week of subcutaneous infusion of 5-HT. These values were compared to the plasma of those receiving TCM alone. 5-HT infusion did not significantly alter blood chemistry between these two groups. Parameters indicative of kidney health including; creatinine (CREA, mg/dL,TCM 0.5±0.1; 5-HT 0.6±0.1), blood urea nitrogen (BUN, mg/dL, TCM 23±0.6; 5-HT 19±5.8), and albumin (ALB, g/dL, TCM 2.7±0.1; 5-HT 2.5±0.2) were not significantly different between 5-HT infused rats and control rats. Liver health was also normal as indicated by no significant change in alanine aminotransferase (ALT, U/L, TCM 64±9.3; 5-HT 55±6.8), alkaline phosphatase (ALKP, U/L, TCM 157±6.1; 5-HT 131±31), and total bilirubin (TBIL, mg/dL, TCM 0.1±0.0; 5-HT 0.1±0.1) values. There was also no impact on metabolic health indicated by no significant difference in cholesterol and triglyceride levels between 5-HT treated rats and control rats (Figure S6). Rats receiving 5-HT showed a significant reduction in body weight (5-HT 422.5±4.6 g, TCM, 438±1.87 g, **, p=0.004) following one week of treatment compared to rats that were not treated with 5-HT.

## 讨论 / Discussion

5-HT is effective at reducing blood pressure chronically in various rodent models of hypertension including the spontaneously hypertensive (SHR) and the deoxycorticosterone acetate (DOCA) salt rat10–12. Our study sought to test the ability of 5-HT to function as an antihypertensive treatment in the presence, and in the absence, of a diuretic (TCM) in rats on two different dietary salt intakes. In animals on normal diet, administration of 5-HT alone resulted in a significant reduction in blood pressure that was not fully sustained. TCM alone caused a slower developing, but sustained hypotension. Co-administration of TCM and 5-HT resulted in a significant and maintained reduction in MAP. In contrast, in animals on high salt intake, TCM alone did not significantly reduce blood pressure but did enhance the hypotensive action of 5-HT alone. In addition to being effective, infusion of 5-HT (with or without TCM) did not modify parameters of kidney, liver, and metabolic health. Our findings show that 5-HT is a safe treatment during 7 days of continuous administration.

5-HT showed augmented hypotensive effects when given together with TCM. This would be expected if the drugs worked via their presumed mechanisms of vasodilation, and fluid volume depletion, respectively. The initial tachycardia observed in rats treated with 5-HT is a hallmark of vasodilation and a result of baroreceptor reflex activation. Furthermore, this is consistent with the knowledge that thiazide diuretics increase the efficacy of nearly all other classes of antihypertensive medications25.

The other question we sought to understand was whether 5-HT maintained its hypotensive properties in a model of hypertension that approaches drug resistance. The Dahl SS rat on 4% NaCl diet was resistant to a thiazide diuretic, one of the drug classes to which some patients are resistant. In the normal salt setting, the rebound in blood pressure during continued 5-HT infusion was probably caused by hypotension induced volume expansion, since hypotension was blunted by treatment with TCM. The ability of TCM to lower blood pressure was less pronounced in the high salt rats (drug resistance). This may be explained by the fact that diuretic treatment is less effective in decreasing blood volume (and thus blood pressure) in a volume-loaded setting. For example, diuretic drug efficacy is reduced in patients on unrestricted salt intake26,27.

The efficacy of 5-HT in lowering blood pressure in the Dahl SS rat on 4% NaCl supports discussion of 5-HT as a treatment for resistant hypertension. The potential of 5-HT lies in its ability as a direct vasodilator. This differs in mechanism from some of the major drug classes that classify resistant hypertension: angiotensin converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers (ARBs) and diuretics28–30. Like 5-HT, long-acting calcium channel blockers are peripheral vasodilators31. However, 5-HT causes vasodilation in large veins and arterioles32–33 while long-acting calcium channel blockers exert their effects primarily in arteries31. Vasodilators are underutilized in the treatment of resistant hypertension34. Our data identify 5-HT as a potential treatment for (resistant) hypertension because of its ability to reduce blood pressure when a current frontline treatment cannot.

We recognize a few limitations of this study. Firstly, that there are many factors of blood pressure regulation that were not incorporated into our study design such as circadian rhythm. Additionally, we only tested the efficacy of 5-HT alongside of and compared to one class of antihypertensive agent. While the use of 5-HT is potentially promising, these experiments should be repeated with the first line antihypertensive therapeutics to which patients are resistant. This would increase our understanding of whether 5-HT could be a potential treatment for resistant hypertension3.

There is currently no true rodent model of resistant hypertension. While many rodent models of hypertension can respond to some type of antihypertensive intervention21, we have not identified nor has the field found a rat model that is resistant to the three frontline medications, the resistance to which defines resistant hypertension.

Finally, 5-HT was administered for a one-week period. This may not be a sufficiently long enough period to truly consider the observed lowering of blood pressure as chronic. With a longer infusion compensatory responses may occur 35. We did not extend the treatment period as other studies have shown the antihypertensive effect of trichlormethiazide to be persistent, and we would expect the same. These data support, however, that future studies would investigate an extended time of co-administration of 5-HT and TCM. We have infused 5-HT for up to 30 days with a continued reduction in blood pressure10.These studies warrant repeating with a longer-term treatment protocol (a month or more).

This study was a small, proof of principle experiment to determine if 5-HT lowered blood pressure in the presence of a first line antihypertensive. Because of this we kept this study to only one sex (M), honoring the three R’s. Supporting the use of one sex in this preliminary experiment, we have yet to find mechanistic differences between the M and F in the blood pressure response to 5-HT24,36. The results of this study will inform our work moving forward that will use both sexes. We appreciate and acknowledge sex as a biological variable and used males only because those were the age matched rats available to us. In regard to outcomes that could be observed if female Dahl SS rats were used, we can speculate as follows. Females, like males, show a reduction in BP when 5-HT is infused. This is true, specifically, for aged female Dahl SS rats (data not shared). Resistance to TCM, the drug used in the present study, has not been compared between M and F head-to-head; we could not readily find evidence that suggests there is a difference diuretic responsiveness in the Dahl SS strain, though there is in response to benzamil in the Sprague Dawley rat (PMID: 31913682). This raises the possibilities that what was observed in the male when 5-HT+ TCM were combined may not be in the female.
